Course Description

This course introduces undergraduate students to the fundamental principles and practices of academic research. Students will learn to navigate scholarly databases, critically evaluate sources, conduct systematic literature reviews, and produce well-structured research papers. Emphasis is placed on developing information literacy, critical thinking skills, and academic writing proficiency essential for success in upper-division coursework and beyond.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  1. Information Literacy: Navigate academic databases and locate appropriate scholarly sources
  2. Critical Analysis: Evaluate the credibility, relevance, and quality of academic sources
  3. Literature Review Skills: Synthesize existing research to identify gaps and patterns in scholarship
  4. Research Design: Formulate focused research questions and develop appropriate methodologies
  5. Academic Writing: Compose clear, well-organized research papers following disciplinary conventions
  6. Citation Practices: Apply proper citation formats (APA, MLA, Chicago) and understand academic integrity principles
  • Textbook: Booth, Wayne C., Gregory G. Colomb, and Joseph M. Williams. The Craft of Research, 4th ed. University of Chicago Press, 2016.
  • Supplemental Readings: Available through D2L course site
  • University of Arizona Library Access: Required for database searches and resource access
  • Citation Management Software: Zotero (free download)

Course Structure

Week 1: Introduction to Academic Research
  • What distinguishes academic research from other forms of inquiry?
  • Overview of the research process
  • Introduction to disciplinary conventions
  • Assignment: Research interest reflection paper (1-2 pages)
Week 2: Information Literacy Fundamentals
  • Evaluating source types: primary vs. secondary sources
  • Understanding peer review process
  • Introduction to UA Library resources
  • Lab: Database navigation workshop
Week 3: Developing Research Questions
  • Moving from topics to focused questions
  • Characteristics of effective research questions
  • Scope and feasibility considerations
  • Assignment: Research question proposal (500 words)
Week 4: Search Strategies
  • Boolean operators and advanced search techniques
  • Subject-specific databases vs. general databases
  • Government documents and grey literature
  • Lab: Advanced database searching
Week 5: Source Evaluation
  • CRAAP test and other evaluation frameworks
  • Recognizing bias and evaluating methodology
  • Currency and relevance in different disciplines
  • Assignment: Source evaluation exercise (5 sources analyzed)
Week 6: Managing Sources
  • Introduction to Zotero citation management
  • Organizing research materials
  • Note-taking strategies for research
  • Lab: Zotero setup and practice
Week 7: Understanding Literature Reviews
  • Purpose and types of literature reviews
  • Literature review vs. annotated bibliography
  • Identifying themes and patterns in scholarship
  • Reading: Sample literature reviews from various disciplines
Week 8: Systematic Review Process
  • Developing search protocols
  • Inclusion and exclusion criteria
  • Creating search logs and documentation
  • Assignment: Literature search plan and initial bibliography (15-20 sources)
Week 9: Synthesizing Sources
  • Moving beyond summary to synthesis
  • Identifying gaps, contradictions, and trends
  • Creating conceptual frameworks
  • Workshop: Synthesis practice with sample sources
Week 10: Writing Literature Reviews
  • Organizational strategies for literature reviews
  • Transitional phrases and coherent flow
  • Balancing breadth and depth
  • Assignment: Literature review draft (1,500-2,000 words)
Week 11: Research Paper Structure
  • Components of academic papers across disciplines
  • Introduction strategies and thesis development
  • Methods sections and research design
  • Peer Review: Literature review drafts
Week 12: Argumentation and Evidence
  • Building persuasive academic arguments
  • Integrating sources effectively
  • Addressing counterarguments and limitations
  • Assignment: Research paper outline and methods section
Week 13: Academic Writing Style
  • Clarity, concision, and precision in academic prose
  • Avoiding plagiarism and maintaining academic integrity
  • Revision strategies for academic writing
  • Workshop: Writing clinic and individual conferences
Week 14: Citation and Documentation
  • In-depth coverage of citation styles (APA focus)
  • Reference list formatting
  • Ethical use of sources
  • Assignment: Complete research paper draft (3,000-4,000 words)
Week 15: Research Presentation
  • Presenting research findings effectively
  • Creating visual aids and handouts
  • Handling questions and feedback
  • Final Presentations: 10-minute research presentations

Major Assignments

Assignment 1: Literature Review (30% of grade)

Due: Week 11

Length: 1,500-2,000 words

Requirements: Systematic review of 15-20 scholarly sources on chosen topic, demonstrating synthesis and critical analysis skills. Must identify research gaps and provide clear organizational framework.

Assignment 2: Research Paper (40% of grade)

Due: Finals Week

Length: 3,000-4,000 words

Requirements: Original research paper incorporating literature review as foundation. Must include proper methodology section, analysis of findings, and discussion of implications. Minimum 20 scholarly sources required.

Assignment 3: Research Presentation (15% of grade)

Due: Week 15

Length: 10 minutes + Q&A

Requirements: Professional presentation of research findings with visual aids. Must demonstrate mastery of subject matter and ability to communicate complex ideas clearly.

Assignment 4: Research Portfolio (15% of grade)

Due: Ongoing throughout semester

Components: Research question proposal, source evaluations, search logs, annotated bibliography, peer review activities, and reflection essays. Portfolio demonstrates research process development.

Academic Integrity

The University of Arizona's Code of Academic Integrity applies to all work in this course. Plagiarism, cheating, and other forms of academic dishonesty will result in failure of the assignment and potentially the course. When in doubt about citation practices, ask for clarification.
